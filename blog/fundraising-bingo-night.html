Fundraising Bingo Night: Step-by-Step Event Plan

A bingo night is a proven fundraising format: low setup cost, high participation, and easy to run in community centers, schools, or clubs. This guide covers planning, printing unique cards, rules, and prize structure.

Plan the event format

Choose 5×5 with a FREE center for mixed ages. Decide round length (8–12 minutes) and number of rounds (6–10). Keep breaks short to maintain energy.

Printing unique cards (fairness)

Always generate a unique card per ticket. If too many people share the same card, you risk multiple winners at once—hard to manage and less fun.

Rules that avoid disputes

  • Define accepted wins: 1 line, 2 lines, X, full card, etc.
  • Decide whether you allow multiple winners per round.
  • Require winners to call “Bingo” immediately and verify quickly.

Prizes and pricing

Offer small prizes each round and one larger prize at the end. Price tickets to cover costs + target donation. Consider bundles (e.g., 3 cards for a discount).

Caller tips

Speak clearly, repeat each number once, and keep a displayed list of called numbers. Use a consistent pace; too fast frustrates beginners, too slow reduces excitement.

Promotion checklist

  • Flyer + social post 2–3 weeks ahead
  • Reminder 3 days before
  • Bring pens/markers, extra printouts, and a verification board
